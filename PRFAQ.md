# Pointer - PRFAQ

## Press Release

### Pointer: The Anti-Meditation App That Points You Back to What You Already Are

**San Francisco, CA** — Today marks the launch of Pointer, a mobile app designed for serious seekers who've grown tired of meditation apps that promise future peace while missing present awareness. Unlike traditional mindfulness apps focused on relaxation or productivity, Pointer delivers direct, non-dual pointings throughout the day—short invitations to recognize the awareness you already are.

"Most meditation apps treat enlightenment like a destination you reach through practice," said [Founder Name]. "Pointer takes the opposite approach. It reminds you that what you're seeking is what's already looking. There's nothing to achieve—only a recognition that keeps being forgotten and remembered."

**The Problem**: Millions use meditation apps seeking inner peace, but remain trapped in the seeker's paradox—the very effort to find peace reinforces the sense of a separate self that needs to find it. Traditional apps gamify meditation with streaks and achievements, inadvertently strengthening the ego they claim to dissolve.

**The Solution**: Pointer delivers 3-5 brief "pointings" daily—questions and observations drawn from Advaita Vedanta, Zen, and contemporary non-dual teachers. Instead of guided meditations, users receive direct inquiries: "What is aware of this moment?" "Can you find the one who's stressed?" "Notice: thoughts are arising, but what are they arising IN?"

**Key Features**:
- **Daily Pointings**: Push notifications with direct inquiries from various non-dual traditions
- **Self-Inquiry Mode**: Guided "Who Am I?" investigation sessions
- **Teacher Lineages**: Curated content from Ramana Maharshi, Nisargadatta, Ashtavakra, Rupert Spira, and more
- **Anti-Progress Design**: No streaks, no gamification, no "meditation minutes"—just recognition
- **Integration Prompts**: Contextual pointings based on time of day (morning clarity, work stress, evening wind-down)

**What Makes Pointer Different**:
1. **Honest about the paradox**: The app acknowledges it's pointing to what can't be taught
2. **Short-form**: 10-30 second engagements, not 20-minute sessions
3. **Multiple traditions**: Advaita, Zen, Dzogchen, contemporary teachers—find what resonates
4. **No spiritual bypassing**: Acknowledges that understanding doesn't instantly rewire conditioning

"After using Pointer for a month, I stopped trying to meditate my way to peace," said a beta tester. "Now I just notice I'm already aware. The app became unnecessary—which I think is the point."

Pointer is available on iOS and Android. Free tier includes 2 daily pointings. Premium ($7.99/month) unlocks unlimited pointings, all teacher lineages, and self-inquiry sessions.

## FAQ

### Customer FAQ

**Q: How is this different from Waking Up or Headspace?**
A: Headspace teaches mindfulness techniques for stress reduction—it's meditation as self-improvement. Waking Up goes deeper with Sam Harris's approach to non-dual awareness. Pointer is more direct and tradition-agnostic. We don't guide you through 10-minute sessions. We interrupt your day with a 10-second question that cuts through whatever story you're believing. Think of us as the friend who keeps asking "but who is the one that's anxious?" until you actually look.

**Q: I'm new to non-duality. Is this for beginners?**
A: Pointer works at any level, but differently. Beginners will find the pointings puzzling at first—"What do you mean 'find the one who's looking'?" That confusion IS the beginning of inquiry. We include introductory content explaining the framework, but we don't water down the pointings. The tradition says the truth is simple enough for anyone to recognize—it just requires looking, not learning.

**Q: What traditions/teachers do you draw from?**
A: We curate from multiple lineages:
- **Advaita Vedanta**: Ramana Maharshi, Nisargadatta Maharaj, Ashtavakra Gita, Papaji
- **Zen**: Direct pointing koans, Huang Po, Bankei
- **Dzogchen**: Pointing-out instructions (adapted)
- **Contemporary**: Rupert Spira, Francis Lucille, Adyashanti, Tony Parsons
- **Original**: Pointings written specifically for modern contexts

**Q: Why no streaks or gamification?**
A: Because gamification reinforces the sense of a separate self accumulating spiritual progress. "I meditated 30 days in a row" strengthens the "I" that non-dual teachings point beyond. We deliberately avoid creating another identity—"the meditator," "the seeker," "the one who's almost enlightened." You're not building toward something. You're recognizing what's already here.

**Q: What's the typical user session like?**
A: You receive a notification: "Before reading this, notice: you're already aware." You read it. Maybe you pause for 5-10 seconds and actually look. Maybe it lands, maybe it doesn't. You continue your day. That's it. We're not asking for 20 minutes of your morning. We're asking for 10 seconds of actual looking, multiple times a day.

**Q: Will this app make me enlightened?**
A: No app, teacher, or practice can make you enlightened because you're already what "enlightenment" points to. The app can only remind you to look—to notice that awareness is already present, already complete. Whether recognition "sticks" isn't up to an app. But consistent pointing can help cut through the habitual forgetting.

**Q: I've tried self-inquiry and just get frustrated. Help?**
A: The frustration IS the practice working. When you ask "Who am I?" and can't find an answer—that failure is the success. The mind's inability to locate the self points directly to your true nature. We include guidance on navigating common traps: turning "I am awareness" into a new belief, using inquiry as another self-improvement project, expecting a special experience.

**Q: Why should I pay when these teachings are free everywhere?**
A: The teachings are indeed freely available. What you're paying for is curation, timing, and integration. We've distilled thousands of pages into potent pointings. We deliver them at moments when you're most likely to actually look. And we've designed the experience to avoid common traps. The Ashtavakra Gita is free online—but will you read it at 2pm when you're stressed about a deadline?

### Internal FAQ (Strategy & Business)

**Q: What's the TAM/SAM/SOM?**
A:
- **TAM**: Global meditation app market ~$5B by 2027
- **SAM**: "Serious seekers" segment—people who've outgrown Headspace, interested in non-duality, read spiritual books. Estimated 5-10% of meditation app users = ~15M globally
- **SOM**: Year 1 target: 50K downloads, 5K paid subscribers ($480K ARR)

**Q: Who is our core customer?**
A: The "post-Headspace seeker":
- Used meditation apps, found them helpful but incomplete
- Read Eckhart Tolle, maybe Nisargadatta or Ramana
- Intellectually understands non-duality but hasn't "stabilized"
- Frustrated by spiritual bypassing and woo-woo content
- Values directness over comfort
- Often professionals (30-50), high education, secular/spiritual-not-religious

**Q: Why will we win against Waking Up?**
A: We're not competing directly—we're complementary. Waking Up is Sam Harris's personal teaching methodology in a polished app. We're a curated pointer system drawing from ALL traditions. Different value props:
- **Waking Up**: Long-form, Harris-centric, theory + practice
- **Pointer**: Micro-format, tradition-agnostic, pure pointing

Some users will use both. Others will prefer our directness and variety.

**Q: What's the monetization strategy?**
A: Freemium:
- **Free**: 2 pointings/day, basic self-inquiry, limited traditions
- **Premium ($7.99/mo, $59.99/yr)**: Unlimited pointings, all traditions, full self-inquiry library, teacher deep-dives, community access

Future:
- Teacher partnerships (revenue share on exclusive content)
- Retreats/events marketplace
- 1:1 mentorship matching

**Q: What's the content moat?**
A:
1. **Curation quality**: Anyone can compile quotes. Our value is selecting the right pointing for the right moment with the right framing
2. **Original content**: Pointings written for modern contexts (work stress, relationship conflict, phone addiction)
3. **Tradition depth**: We respect lineages while making them accessible
4. **Anti-hype positioning**: Our honest, no-BS tone builds trust with skeptical seekers

**Q: What are the key risks?**
A:
1. **Market size uncertainty**: "Serious seekers" may be smaller than estimated
2. **Content licensing**: Some teachers may not want content in apps
3. **The success paradox**: If the app works, users stop needing it
4. **Differentiation clarity**: May be confused with meditation apps

**Q: How do we handle the "success paradox"?**
A: Embrace it. Our marketing acknowledges: "The goal of this app is to become unnecessary." This honesty builds trust and differentiates us. Practically, even after recognition, people appreciate ongoing pointings—like a friend who reminds you of what you know. Churn will be higher than Headspace—that's fine. We optimize for depth of impact over stickiness.

## Appendix: Sample Pointings

**Morning**
- "Before you check your phone: What is already awake?"
- "Notice: you didn't start being aware. Awareness was here before the first thought."

**Work Stress**
- "The deadline is real. The one who's stressed about it—can you find them?"
- "Thoughts about the problem are arising. What are they arising in?"

**Evening**
- "The day happened. But did it happen TO you, or AS you?"
- "Rest doesn't come from stopping. Notice what has never been disturbed."

**Self-Inquiry**
- "Ask: 'Who am I?' Don't answer with a thought. What remains when no answer comes?"
- "Turn attention back to its source. What is looking?"

**Ashtavakra-style**
- "You are not the body. The body is not yours. You are not the doer. What are you?"
- "Bondage is believing you can be bound. Freedom is seeing you were never the one who could be."

**Zen-style**
- "What was your face before your parents were born?"
- "If you meet the Buddha on the road, who is walking?"
